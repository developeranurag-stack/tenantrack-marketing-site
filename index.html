Frequently asked questions

What is TenanTrack?

TenanTrack is rent tracking software for small landlords in the US. It organizes your properties, units, and tenants, generates a month-by-month rent schedule for every lease, applies late fees automatically, sends rent reminders, and exports a tax-ready record of every payment at year end.

Do I need to be tech-savvy to use it?

No. TenanTrack is built for landlords, not accountants or IT staff. You add a property, add a unit, add a tenant, and set up a lease — the rest (rent due dates, late fees, reminders) runs on its own.

How does TenanTrack handle late rent and late fees?

You set a grace period and a late fee rule (flat dollar amount or a percentage of rent) once, per lease. If rent isn't paid by the due date plus the grace period, TenanTrack marks the charge late and applies the fee automatically — you never have to calculate or remember it yourself.

Will my tenants get reminded to pay rent automatically?

Yes. TenanTrack reminds tenants before rent is due, the day after it's due, and again the moment a late fee is applied — by email automatically, with SMS and phone-call reminders you can trigger yourself in one click.

Can I use TenanTrack if I only rent out one property?

Yes. The Free plan covers a single unit at no cost, and you can upgrade as your portfolio grows — plans scale from one unit up to unlimited units for larger portfolios.

Does TenanTrack help with tax season?

Yes. TenanTrack can export every rent charge for a given year as a single file, ready to hand to your accountant or use for your own tax prep, so you're not digging through bank statements every spring.

How much does TenanTrack cost?

Plans start free for a single unit, with paid tiers at $9.99, $19.99, and $39.99 per month as your portfolio grows, and a custom-scale plan for portfolios over 25 units. See the Pricing section above for full details.
